## Question

Does changing the **labeling modulus** from classic vortex math’s mod 9 (and related composites such as 37, 111, 333) create new structure when unit-circle points are generated by an **irrational rotation** (default step \(9/\pi\), or coupled step \(m/\pi\))?

In particular: is the visual “symmetry” under \(m=37\) a form of **angle–label locking**, or something else?

## Three-layer picture (supported)

| Phenomenon | Strongest modulus | Evidence | **Not** the same as |
|------------|-------------------|----------|---------------------|
| **Algebraic cleanness** | **37** | Long clean \(\times 2\) cycle (len 36 from 1; only 2 cycles on \(\mathbb{Z}/37\mathbb{Z}\)); 2 is a primitive root mod 37 | Label–angle lock |
| **Orderly label flow along the orbit** | **111** (often, under \(m/\pi\)) | Highest **label progression** / symmetry score when step is coupled to modulus | Statistical angle lock |
| **True positional angle lock** | **`angle_bin` only** (by design) | Strongly **positive exNMI**, huge \(z\) vs shuffle null | Natural discovery under `step_index` |

### Algebra (37 family)

| \(m\) | \(\times 2\) len from 1 | # cycles (full structure) | Note |
|------|-------------------------|---------------------------|------|
| 9 | 6 | 3 | Classic vortex 1-2-4-8-7-5 + Trinity residues |
| 37 | 36 | 2 | Near-Hamiltonian nonzero orbit |
| 111 \(=3\times 37\) | 36 | 5 | Same long orbit length; CRT fragmentation |
| 333 \(=9\times 37\) | 36 | 12 | Heavily fragmented |

111 is the **prime-mover composite** (\(R_3=111=3\times 37\)); 37 is its **repunit prime** (\(\mathrm{ord}_{37}(10)=3\)).

### Progression vs lock (metrics)

- **Angular uniformity** stays high (~0.95–0.99) for dense orbits (\(n\sim 500\)) — sanity check, not discriminative.
- **Label progression** moves most under coupled \(m/\pi\); **111** often leads.
- **Raw NMI** is **cardinality-biased** (more labels → higher NMI without true lock).
- **exNMI** = observed NMI − mean NMI under label shuffle → fair across moduli.

### Control: `angle_bin` vs `step_index`

| Method | exNMI | \(z\) | Meaning |
|--------|-------|-------|---------|
| `angle_bin` | Strongly positive (~+0.1 to +0.72) | \(\sim 60\sigma\)–\(170\sigma\) | Real angle–label dependence; **null baseline works** |
| `step_index` | Negative (~−0.005 to −0.12) | Negative | **No** angle lock beyond (or below) chance |

Exactly what is expected from an **irrational rotation** labeled by **step index**: the flow has no preferred angles unless the labeling method introduces angular bins by construction.

## Defensible narrative takeaway

> Changing the modulus from 9 to other values **does not** create statistical angle–label locking under step-index labeling on an irrational rotation.
>
> What it **does** change is:
>
> 1. The **algebraic structure** of the doubling map (**37** wins cleanly).  
> 2. How **orderly labels progress** along the geometric orbit when the step size is coupled to the modulus (**111** often wins under \(m/\pi\)).
>
> Apparent visual “symmetry” under `step_index` is **progression/order**, not **positional locking**. The `angle_bin` control confirms the statistics can detect real locking when it exists.

This is sharper and more interesting than “mod 37 is special in every way.”
